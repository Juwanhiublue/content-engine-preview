hiublue AI Marketing Engine: Team Pitch

Prepared by Juwan (Product) · For: Ralph + the team · 2026-06-06

The one-liner

An AI marketing department that runs a business's entire demand engine, organic content, email, SMS, and paid ads, off one strategy and one measurement loop, without the founder's time. We build it for hiublue first, then replicate it to other businesses as a second revenue line.

1. The problem (we are living it)

hiublue has a strong product but no engine for demand. Ralph is the founder, CEO, and the face of the brand, the marketing only works when it comes from him, and he has no time to create it. The result: no consistent brand awareness and no reliable leads in the pipeline.

We have already proven the root cause the expensive way:

  • We hired an agency. The content stopped the moment Ralph couldn't give it time.
  • We hired a sales team. It failed because there were no leads coming in to close.

The bottleneck is top-of-funnel demand, and the one person who can create it is out of time. In 6 months, if nothing changes, revenue does not grow and jobs are at risk. Marketing is the make-or-break lever, and right now it is not running.

This is not a hiublue-only problem. Most businesses in our ICP have the same shape: the founder is the face, and the founder has no time.

2. The bet

If we build a system that produces and runs a founder's marketing across every channel without the founder's time, we fix hiublue's growth problem AND create a product we can sell to every other business with the same problem.

We are our own first customer, the best possible proof. Build it to save hiublue, and it proves itself before we ever pitch an outsider.

3. Who it's for

The hiublue ICP, narrowed to the slice that feels this most: founder- or owner-led businesses where the founder is the face of the brand but has no time to be consistent. Agencies, coaches & creators, founders & solopreneurs, real estate, local services, recruiters, and the rest of the "lives or dies by outbound and replies" list.

4. The product: the AI Marketing Engine

Intake a business once, and the engine runs every channel on autopilot, guided by what is actually converting. Every channel runs the same loop:

KNOWLEDGE → RESEARCH (what's winning) → CREATE the asset → REVIEW (human gate) → DEPLOY → MEASURE ↺

5. Why hiublue wins (the wedge)

  • We already are a multi-channel AI worker. hiublue's core product already does email, SMS, DMs, and outreach. We are not bolting on channels we do not have, we already own the hardest part. This engine = a content brain + our existing outreach worker, wired into one demand machine. No generic AI tool can say that.
  • It is on-brand and believable from us. "A worker that runs your marketing" is exactly what hiublue stands for. We are dogfooding our own promise.
  • It compounds. The measurement loop makes month six smarter than month one.

6. The repeatable process (weekly)

The operator's entire week: pick the week's themes (guided by last week's winners) and approve what ships. Under an hour a day. Everything else is automated. Because measurement feeds back into research, the system improves itself every week instead of guessing.

Human touch points = pick themes, tweak, approve. That is it.

7. How it scales (the replication layer = second revenue)

  • Built once (fixed forever): the engine, the pipeline, the render templates, the engineered-prompt standard, the integrations. Our devs build this for hiublue.
  • The only thing that changes per business: a config, knowledge base, avatar + voice + consent, brand kit, channels, cadence, audience, CTA.

Onboarding business #2 = run the intake checklist, drop in their config, run the exact same loop. No new engineering per client. Dev effort is front-loaded once and amortized across every business. That is how a fix for our own problem becomes a product line.

9. Who runs it (the PM role)

Juwan owns the outcome and the quality, not the manual work, the engine does that. The role grows in three phases:

  • Build (now → first results): PM driving the dev team, owning the spec, the timeline, and the inputs (Ralph's assets, knowledge base, brand kit). First operator, finds what breaks, feeds it back.
  • Operate (once live): Monday, pick the week's themes from the scoreboard. Midweek, review + approve. Daily, a quick check + glance at numbers. Friday, read results, double down on winners. Under an hour a day on execution; the rest on strategy.
  • Scale (clients come): owner of the product line, onboard businesses, own client success, steer the roadmap.

The engine automates production. It does not automate taste, strategy, the approval call, relationships, or accountability for results. That is the job: operator and owner of hiublue's marketing engine, head of growth powered by a system instead of a headcount.

Juwan's day to day

Build phase (now to first results):

  • Morning: stand-up with the devs, unblock them, keep the 60-day plan on track; turn the vision into prioritized specs for the week.
  • Midday: gather the system's inputs (Ralph's photo, voice, and consent; the knowledge base; the brand kit).
  • Afternoon: first operator. Run the loop end to end, find what breaks, feed it back to the devs; set the creative bar for what "good" looks like.
  • Ongoing: report progress to Ralph and the team. Single owner of the timeline.

Operate phase (once live), a typical week:

  • Monday (~45 min): open the scoreboard, see what won last week, pick the week's themes and angles across channels.
  • Tue/Wed (~30 min): review the generated scripts, emails, and SMS; tweak the voice; approve the videos or send them back.
  • Daily (~15 min): confirm posts and sends went out; glance at early numbers.
  • Friday (~30 min): read the week's results, double down on winners, kill losers, hand the devs the next improvement.

Under an hour a day on execution. The rest of the day is the high-value work: strategy, creative direction, and owning whether the funnel is actually filling.

10. The plan (staged, 60-day proof)

Big vision, disciplined first step. We do NOT build all channels at once.

  • Phase 1: the 60-day proof (owned channels): organic content + email. Zero ad-spend risk, fastest path to consistent output and first leads. Prove the loop and the measurement on hiublue itself.
  • Phase 2: SMS: cheap to add, hiublue's worker already sends it.
  • Phase 3: paid ads: last, needs budget and carries risk, and by then it just reuses the creative we are already making.

Same engine throughout. Channels switch on as modules.

11. Success metrics (what "results in 60 days" means)

Awareness and leads do not move overnight, so we set targets we can actually hit and measure:

  • Primary (60 days): consistent multi-channel output live without Ralph's time, and first attributable engagement + lead signal trending up.
  • Secondary: reach, follower growth, email/SMS engagement as leading indicators.
  • System health: time from idea to published; % auto-handled vs. manual; time to onboard a new business (the scale metric).
  • Do NOT promise: "pipeline full" in 60 days. That is the trap that makes a working system look like a failure.

12. The ask

  • Team: a small pod from our developer team to build the engine once (orchestration, render templates, integrations), plus Juwan as PM/operator.
  • Time: 60 days to first results on hiublue.
  • Budget: primarily tools we already have or access (HeyGen, Buffer, AI, hiublue worker, dev time). Paid-ads budget is Phase 3 only, decided later.
  • Inputs from Ralph: photo + voice + written consent for his AI persona, and ~the knowledge base.
